# Frame challenges — folder-shape depth bug fix (item 2)

## Frame as given

The orchestrator's frame: item 2 is the long tail of a structural migration, not a standalone chore. The "honest question" is reframed to optimize for *consistency with item 1's regime + recurrence reduction*, with a hard precondition that item 1 commit before item 2 ships. The reframe pulls in regime-check, scope-check, and recurrence-check as first-class concerns, names "migration durability" as the unsurfaced alternative optimization, and treats every `../`-chain link in the repo as a latent bug. Bundling item 3 with item 2, extending to two newly-surfaced files (`living-poweruser-knowledge-module` L48 and the mixed `r-and-d-lab-thesis`), and gating commit on item 1 all fall out of this frame.

## Alternative frame

**The reframe is a frame of "regime collision; eliminate the latent bug class." A different frame is: this is housekeeping that the punch-list already correctly sized, and the meta-system (the 12-step pipeline being run on it) is the real subject under examination.** The verb shifts from *eliminate* to *honor* — honor the punch-list's narrowness as a pre-committed scope contract authored by the operator in a calmer moment, and treat every "while we're here" extension as a violation of that contract regardless of how cheap or coherent the extension looks. Under this frame the optimization target is **operator attention preservation across the full 13-item punch-list**, not migration durability of a 30-link substrate. Items 4–13 are unread by every agent in this session; they may contain decisions whose value-per-minute dwarfs anything item 2 produces. The frame's downstream answer is: do the literal punch-list line, do not bundle item 3, do not extend to newly-found files, do not block on item 1, ship the smallest diff that closes the line, and route the latent-bug-class question to a separate (later, possibly never) decision. The recurrence argument is acknowledged and explicitly deferred.

## Condition under which the current frame is wrong

The current frame is wrong if no third folder-shape migration is anticipated within the punch-list horizon — because the entire "migration durability" optimization is purchased against a recurrence event that may never fire, and the cost of the wider sweep (Option A bundled, two extensions, item-1 dependency) is paid up front in attention spent on items 2-3 instead of items 4-13.

## Challenges to scope-map bundling and extension decisions

The scope-map preserves four primitives (5, 6, 7, 8) but the preservation reasons there are defensible (rule text is item 1's territory; PASS files are correct as-is; sanctity rule is explicit policy; `/upgrade` is item 1's tooling surface). The sharper challenges are to the *bundling* and *extension* decisions, which the scope-map default-routed to subsume/extend without enough push-back:

- **Bundling item 3 into item 2 (Primitive 3, subsume).** Stated reason: two of three item-3 occurrences are in the same files as item-2 targets, third is one line, splitting produces near-identical commits. That reason is weak because *colocation in source files is not the boundary that matters for punch-list bookkeeping* — the boundary that matters is whether item 3 was authored as a separate line for a reason the bundling discards. The punch-list author wrote items 2 and 3 as distinct lines; the bundling assumes they were split for taxonomic neatness rather than for sequencing or risk-isolation. The bundling biases the downstream answer toward **author-time scope expansion masquerading as edit-time efficiency** — the savings are measured in keystrokes, the cost is measured in the punch-list's invariant that one line = one decision.

- **Extending to `living-poweruser-knowledge-module/README.md` L48 (Primitive 2, extend).** Stated reason: "exclusion would knowingly leave a Explore-confirmed broken link in active surface." That reason is weak because *Explore's discovery scope was not the punch-list's scope* — Explore was asked to audit; the punch-list author had already chosen to name two files. "Knowingly leaving a broken link" assumes the operator is unaware; if the operator already knew (the punch-list precedes Explore), then "while we're here" is precisely the discipline the punch-list format exists to refuse. The extension biases the downstream answer toward **agent-discovered scope > human-authored scope**, which inverts the priority the punch-list encodes.

- **Extending to `r-and-d-lab-thesis/README.md` (mixed, Primitive 11, extend).** Stated reason: "same edit class; excluding it leaves a known partial-broken file in active surface." Same critique as above, sharper: a *mixed* file is evidence the prior author already partially corrected it, which means the file has been touched and left in this state deliberately or through partial attention. Sweeping it under item 2 erases that signal. The extension biases the downstream answer toward **uniformity over fidelity to whatever editorial state existed before**.

- **Gating commit on item 1 (Primitive 4, conflict, "block item 2 commit on item 1 commit").** Stated reason: item 2's correct fix-form differs between options; cannot proceed under both. That reason is weak because *item 1 is not the only unit of decision available* — item 2 itself, executed as a literal `../` → `../../../` mechanical fix under option (b), would *demonstrate the cost of option (b)* (15 link edits to compensate for one structural change) and force item 1's hand toward option (a) on evidence rather than on plan-recommendation. The gating direction may be inverted: item 2 should pressure item 1 by exposing the cost of ambiguity, not wait on item 1 to resolve the ambiguity in advance. The gating biases the downstream answer toward **plan-before-evidence**, which is exactly the failure mode item-1's plan recommends against in its own postmortem framing.

## Challenge to the classifier label

The classifier's primary label is `refactor` with `migrate` as conditional secondary. The alternative classification (`migrate`) becomes substantively different under option (a) + bundling: at that point the work is a convention migration with two-systems-running concerns (any reader/tool depending on the old click-resolution breaks at the cutover) and the done-condition shifts from "links click" to "links are repo-root-relative and grep-stable." But there is a *third* classification the requirement.md did not surface: **`investigation`**. The reframe's "scope check" and "recurrence check" steps are investigatory, not refactor work. If item 2 is partially an investigation into how many latent bugs exist and whether folder reshapes recur, then *that* sub-work should be split out and named — not folded into a refactor diff. The frame as given absorbs investigatory work under a refactor label and ships it as a single commit, which loses the audit trail that an investigation deliverable would produce.

## Frame-level objection the orchestrator should carry forward

**Running the 12-step pipeline on a 30-link mechanical fix may itself be the failure mode — process for process's sake — and the generator must defend why this item warrants the full apparatus rather than a literal punch-list-line execution that preserves operator attention for items 4–13.**

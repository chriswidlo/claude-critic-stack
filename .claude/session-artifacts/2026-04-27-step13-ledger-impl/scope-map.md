## Conflicts (ranked)

1. **The CLAUDE.md "Step 12 synthesis is the final operator-facing output" implicit contract.** The entry proposes Step 13 *after* synthesis. Resolution: the ledger is *operator-internal-facing*, written *for* the synthesis to cite. The synthesis remains the user-facing terminus; the ledger is its appendix. Rename to "Step 13 — ledger appendix" if needed for clarity. **Resolved.**

2. **The "ratios as data" temptation.** Storing computed ratios alongside raw counts invites drift if a session has 0 decisions (division by zero) or if the schema later changes definitions. Resolution: counts are stored; ratios are presented in human-readable form ("14 calls / 1 decision = 14") so the math is visible and re-derivable. Do *not* store ratios as separate rows. **Resolved.**

3. **Counting "decisions" is fuzzy.** A synthesis names 1 explicit recommendation but contains many sub-decisions. Resolution: define "decision" = "the single explicit recommendation in synthesis.md, plus any explicitly-named alternatives the operator must choose between." Document this in the template. **Resolved by template definition.**

4. **Loop-cap-reached vs converged-at-loop-2.** Both report "loops = 2" but mean different things. Resolution: ledger has a separate `loop_cap_reached` boolean derived from decision-log.md content (matching "Loops used: 2/2" style). **Resolved.**

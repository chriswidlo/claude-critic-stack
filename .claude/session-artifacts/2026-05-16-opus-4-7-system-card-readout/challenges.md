# Frame challenges — Opus 4.7 system-card readout

## Frame as given
The orchestrator (frame.md Revision 1) reframed the user's "deepest possible adversarial readout + concrete change-set" into "operator update under vendor self-report uncertainty," optimizing for audit-grade traceability and a decision-bearing change-set, with adversarial reading as the defense against vendor anchoring. The frame commits to verbatim citation discipline, treats the system card as the best primary source on inaccessible model behavior, and binds zone-readers to invariants tied to candidate repo changes. 4.7 is treated as a fresh baseline (no 4.6 delta), with the outside-view step asked to do extra work to compensate.

## Alternative frame
**The system card should not reshape the repo at all.** Recast the question as: *"Given a single vendor self-report whose reference class (per the outside-view distillation) sits below base rate and whose corpus coverage is declared thin, what is the smallest possible repo response — possibly null — that is still defensible?"* The optimization target flips from "decision-bearing change-set" to **"defensibly-null change-set unless a finding crosses an a-priori threshold the repo set before reading the card."** Verb change: not *translate findings into changes*, but *triage findings against a pre-committed change-bar*. Under this frame, the right outcome of a diligent readout may be a one-paragraph synthesis that says "no repo primitive moves; here is the threshold that was not crossed and the post-deployment signal that would cross it." The current frame cannot produce that outcome: it has pre-committed to *some* change-set existing, which is a structural bias toward producing changes regardless of evidence weight. The outside-view distillation already tells you the dominant failure mode of vendor self-reports is *gradient erosion observable only post-deployment*; a frame that lets pre-deployment reading drive repo changes is reading off the wrong end of the evidence curve.

## Condition under which the current frame is wrong
The current frame is wrong if the change-set the workflow produces is substantially indistinguishable from one that could be written by reading the system card's executive summary alone — i.e., if the `audit-grade traceability + adversarial reading` machinery did not change *which* primitives moved, only *how thoroughly cited* the movement is. In that case the frame produced epistemic theater: a thoroughly-quoted dossier whose conclusions did not require the dossier to exist.

## Challenges to scope-map preservations
`scope-map.md` does not exist on disk at the time of this challenge. Per contract, no preservation challenges are written here. **However**, the absence of a scope-map at the gate the frame-challenger normally runs is itself a frame risk: when scope-map lands later, the orchestrator must re-invoke this challenger or carry forward the standing rule that **any preservation justified by "still works," "no 4.7 finding directly contradicts it," or "absence of a triggering finding"** is rejected on its face. "Still works" is the lowest-rigor preservation reason in this stack and is the default route to producing zero work while *appearing* to have considered change. If scope-map preserves a primitive on those grounds, the orchestrator must either (a) name the specific 4.7 finding that the primitive *would have moved on, had it been present*, and explain why its absence is informative rather than uninformative, or (b) reclassify the row as `subsume` or `replace` and force the generator to do the work.

## Challenge to the classifier label
The classifier landed `investigation` primary, `new` secondary, with `extend` as the alternative. The user's framing words — "deepest possible adversarial readout," "concrete change-set," "treat as primary risk dossier" — pull the frame toward *production* (a dossier, a change-set, a canon ingest). Under the alternative classification `extend`, the downstream frame would shift meaningfully: the readout would be scoped to "what 4.7 evidence amends an *existing* contract clause," with the burden of proof on demonstrating that an existing primitive's contract is *insufficient* under a 4.7 finding. That burden is heavier than the current frame's implicit "what new primitive does 4.7 motivate," which permits net-new agents and rules to be added on the strength of "the card says X and X is interesting." If the secondary were primary, the change-set would likely shrink by half and would not include net-new canon entries derived from a single document — which the corpus-thinness flag in the canon-librarian distillation suggests is the more defensible move.

## Challenge to the dropped 4.6 delta
The user explicitly dropped the 4.6 delta and the orchestrator accepted that constraint, asking outside-view to compensate. The outside-view distillation did *not* compensate — it produced a reference-class verdict ("below base rate") and a failure-mode narrative (gradient erosion), neither of which substitutes for "what is new in 4.7 versus what has been true since 4.5." Without a delta, the readout cannot tell the reader whether the 12% → 33% AI-safety-R&D refusal regression is a 4.7-specific event or a continuation of a trend visible across 4.5 and 4.6 — and the change-set's per-finding weighting is structurally unable to distinguish "new behavior the repo has never seen" from "behavior the repo has been silently coexisting with for two model generations." If the resulting change-set is approximately what an Opus 4.6 readout would have produced, the readout did not earn its keep, and the dropped-delta decision should be revisited before synthesis rather than after.

## Frame-level objection the orchestrator should carry forward
The generator must demonstrate that the change-set it proposes would *not* have been produced by reading the system card's executive summary alone — and must name the specific finding-to-primitive bridge for each proposed change such that, if the finding were retracted, the change would also be retracted; otherwise the workflow has produced epistemic theater that the in-family critic-panel is structurally unlikely to catch.

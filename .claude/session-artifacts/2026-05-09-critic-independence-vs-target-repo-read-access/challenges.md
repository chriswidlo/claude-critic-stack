# Challenges

## Frame as given

The orchestrator has framed the question as a tension along the "independence" axis: critics reading the target repo introduces shared input that may correlate their verdicts, so the panel must choose how much to *isolate* (preserve / restrict / isolate / formalize) versus how much to *ground*. The frame names three independence sub-axes (inter-lens, inter-model, inter-input), points out that the candidate may already leak target-repo content, and reserves the option to "drop the independence-of-information claim" but does not commit to it. Implicit optimization: preserve the panel's *decorrelation* property while admitting the inputs are partly shared.

## Alternative frame

**The panel does not have an independence problem; it has a verdict-commensurability problem.** Reframe the question from "are the critics independent?" to "are the three lenses answering the same question, and is the minority-veto rule averaging answers of the same kind?" Under this frame, the load-bearing axis is not *correlation between verdicts* but *type-equivalence of verdicts*: a lens that has read the target repo answers "is this candidate a good move *for that target*?" and a lens that has not answers "is this candidate sound *on its own terms*?" Those are not noisy estimates of one underlying quantity; they are different quantities. No amount of input-isolation, model-decorrelation, or role-orthogonality fixes a panel whose three outputs are not commensurable in the first place. The verb shifts from "decorrelate" to "type-check": before discussing isolation, declare per-lens what the verdict claims, and then ask whether veto across non-equivalent verdicts is meaningful arithmetic.

## Condition under which the current frame is wrong

The current frame is wrong if the dominant correlation source is the candidate.md narrative the orchestrator authors at step 9 — because in that case every downstream "independence" intervention (per-lens isolation, shadow comparator, role-orthogonality) operates on the *output* side of a correlation pipeline whose *input* node is upstream of the panel and is unmeasured.

## Challenges to scope-map preservations

- **Three-lens critic panel structure (architecture / operations / product), preservation reason was canon's role-orthogonality argument plus outside-view's relational/structural-independence finding.** That reason is weak because both citations support role-orthogonality as *a* mechanism — neither establishes it is *sufficient* in the presence of a shared candidate.md. Outside-view's "zero-of-four reference classes use information-blinding" argues *against* isolation, not *for* the lens-triple as the right decorrelation primitive; canon's PRR analogue is a single-team grounded review, which actually argues against parallel decomposition in this regime (canon §10 explicitly flags shared-context tasks as a poor multi-agent fit). Preserving the triple by citing these passages selects the half of each source that supports preservation while ignoring the half that questions whether parallel critic decomposition is the right architecture at all. The preservation biases the downstream answer toward *adding new mechanisms on top of the panel* (per-lens job declarations, pass-through tables) rather than toward the more disruptive question of whether the panel as currently constituted is the right shape.

- **Shadow comparator and `SHADOW_PANEL=1` gating, preservation reason was orthogonal correlation source (inter-model vs. inter-input).** That reason is weak because "addresses a different correlation source" is a statement about *coverage*, not about *value under the new requirement*. If the dominant correlation source turns out to be shared candidate.md framing (Explore §5, gap §6), then the shadow comparator's inter-model decorrelation operates downstream of the binding constraint and contributes near-zero information about the verdict's reliability. Preserving it without re-justifying its marginal value under the new threat model biases toward *additive complexity* — keeping every existing decorrelation mechanism and stacking new ones — rather than toward asking which mechanisms are still load-bearing once the dominant correlation source is named.

- **Minority-veto rule (preserved implicitly inside the lens-triple row).** The scope-map preserves minority-veto by extending the panel rather than replacing it, but does not separately defend the veto rule. The reason is weak because veto presumes the three verdicts are commensurable — three "approve / rework / reject" labels of the same kind. If per-lens job declaration is added (calibrated grounded judgment vs. abstract ungrounded judgment), one lens's "reject" may mean "incompatible with the deployed target" while another's "reject" means "logically unsound on its own terms." A single `reject` from either then triggers veto with identical force despite being claims about different worlds. Preservation biases toward *retaining the aggregation rule* even as the inputs to that rule become typed differently; the alternative — replacing veto with a typed-aggregation rule, or admitting the panel produces three independent reports rather than one verdict — is taken off the table by labelling the row `extend`.

- **Path-discipline regime, preservation reason was non-negotiable per CLAUDE.md.** The preservation itself is fine, but framing it as a primitive in this scope-map biases the answer: it implies the new requirement *layers on top of* path-discipline, when in fact path-discipline addresses a privacy concern (no absolute paths in artifacts) and is silent on the *information-content* concern (which lens may dereference `<target>/...`). Treating it as a relevant primitive risks importing path-discipline's "non-negotiable" tone into a question where the right answer may be that some lenses must not dereference the path at all — a stronger constraint than path-discipline carries today. The preservation biases toward *path-rendering solutions* (rewrites, gitignored files) when the question is about *information access*.

## Challenge to the classifier label

The classifier marked this `investigation` and flagged `extend` as a possible alternative if the user commits to "add an information-isolation primitive." The substantive alternative the classifier did not develop is `replace`: the user's framing words ("are we compromising the independence … allowing critics … to read the destination/target repo") presume independence is the load-bearing property *and* presume critics currently lack target-repo read access. Explore established that critics currently have unconstrained `Read` and indirect target-repo exposure via candidate.md / question.md; if that grounding is already de-facto load-bearing rather than optional, the right label is `replace` — the implicit grounding model is replaced by an explicit one — not `extend`. Under `replace`, the downstream frame would not be "preserve, restrict, isolate, or formalize" (a menu over an assumed-optional access decision), but "the panel's independence claim is currently a story the stack tells about itself; what claim should it tell instead?" That is a `replace` question about the *justification* of the panel, and frame.md's option (5) — accept grounding, drop the information-independence claim, re-justify on role-orthogonality + shadow-comparator triangulation — becomes the natural answer rather than the uncomfortable fifth option. The classifier's `investigation` framing keeps the menu intact and lets the panel's current self-description survive without being directly contested; the `replace` framing forces the contest.

## Frame-level objection the orchestrator should carry forward

The current frame treats "independence" as the load-bearing axis and asks how to defend it; the generator must address the possibility that independence is the wrong organizing concept entirely — that the three lens verdicts are not commensurable post-grounding, that the dominant correlation source is the orchestrator-authored candidate.md upstream of every panel mechanism, and that frame.md option (5) ("accept that critics are grounded, drop the independence-of-information claim, re-justify on role-orthogonality + shadow-comparator triangulation") may be the only honest position rather than an uncomfortable alternative.

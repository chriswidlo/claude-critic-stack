# Architecture critic — verdict for `2026-04-27-critics-get-write-tool-impl`

## 1. Weakest structural link

**The proposal collapses two roles onto the same agent within the same invocation: critic-as-reviewer (verdict-producer) and critic-as-author-of-record (artifact-writer), without any structural separation between the act of judging and the act of recording the judgment.** Today, those roles live on different sides of a chokepoint: the critic returns prose, the orchestrator persists. That chokepoint is not incidental — it is the place where path-discipline, session-id binding, and write-target invariants are validated by a *single* component (the orchestrator) rather than three. Granting `Write` to each critic does not "extend a tool grant"; it dissolves the role boundary. The instruction-level defenses ("write only to `<id>/critiques/<lens>.md`", "do not Read sibling files") are *prose contracts on the same agent that is being asked to produce a high-stakes judgment in the same turn* — i.e., the agent under cognitive load is the agent enforcing its own write-scope. The Write-holder precedent (`subagent-distiller`, `scope-mapper`, `frame-challenger`) does not refute this because those agents have *one* job (produce-and-persist a single artifact). The critics now have *two* jobs that pull on different attention surfaces: render a lens-specific judgment, and observe a write-path contract. This is the structural fact most likely to fail in production, and the candidate addresses it only by analogy ("uniform with sequential Write-holders") which is exactly the analogy the frame-challenger broke.

## 2. Invariants at risk

(These are invariants the candidate does *not* name. A1–A4 in the candidate name *assumptions*, not invariants — different category.)

1. **Session-id monotonicity within a panel invocation.** The three critics are invoked in parallel against the same session-id. Nothing in the proposal binds the session-id used in the critic's prompt to the path the critic writes — they are two independent string interpolations. Invariant statement: *for every file landing under `<id>/critiques/<lens>.md`, `<id>` equals the orchestrator's currently-active session-id at the moment of the panel invocation.* Violation: a critic that mis-templates (or that the orchestrator mis-templates *to*) writes to a stale session, contaminating a closed audit, and no part of the proposal detects this. The candidate's "post-hoc directory-read" detection assumes the operator notices an extra file in a session they have already closed — an unbounded-latency detector.

2. **Write-once-per-lens-per-session.** Today, `critiques.md` is a single artifact rewritten by the orchestrator; idempotence is trivial. Under the new contract, the rewrite-loop in Step 11 produces a contract gap (the candidate inherits Explore's §Gap 3 unresolved): does a re-invoked critic overwrite `<lens>.md` or write `<lens>-v2.md`? The candidate ships without resolving this. Invariant: *exactly one current verdict per (session, lens) is readable by Step 12.* Violation: Step 12 reads a stale v1 and a fresh v2 from different lenses and produces an incoherent synthesis.

3. **Ballot non-observability before commit.** The candidate names this invariant ("do not Read siblings before writing") and tries to enforce it instructionally. The invariant *that breaks regardless of instruction compliance* is: *no critic's working context contains any byte authored by another critic in the same panel turn.* Today this invariant holds by construction — there is no path from critic-A's output into critic-B's input. Under the new shape, the path exists (filesystem) and the only barrier is a sentence in the agent body. The invariant is now enforced by *agent self-discipline*, which is a category weaker than enforcement-by-construction. The candidate explicitly accepts this trade; what it does not name is that the invariant has changed *type*, not just enforcement strength.

4. **Single-author-per-artifact (audit chain integrity).** An audit artifact is trustworthy when its provenance is unambiguous. The candidate creates a class of files (`critiques/<lens>.md`) that are *sometimes* critic-authored (going forward) and *sometimes* orchestrator-authored (precedent files in two prior sessions, per Explore §Direct facts 9–10). The candidate does not propose a marker distinguishing the two. Invariant: *for any `critiques/<lens>.md` on disk, the reader can determine its author without reading sibling files or git history.* Violation: a future operator/agent reading a mixed-precedent corpus cannot tell which files are paraphrase and which are original.

## 3. Coupling and direction

- **Direction inversion.** Stable component (orchestrator, the chokepoint that owns path-discipline) currently depends on volatile components (critic outputs as prose strings). The proposal flips the direction: the orchestrator now depends on the *filesystem state produced by* the critics. Volatile-to-stable becomes stable-depends-on-volatile-via-IO — an inversion the candidate never names.
- **New filesystem-mediated coupling between siblings.** Critics A, B, C were previously coupled only through the orchestrator (a star topology). They are now coupled through a shared directory (`critiques/`), enabling sibling-read by tool capability even if instruction forbids it. Star topology becomes mesh-via-filesystem. This is a coupling-direction regression.
- **Layering violation in CLAUDE.md.** The new Step 11 rule ("replan-on-self-modification") couples Step 11's replan logic to the *content* of Step 7's scope-map (specifically, paths matching `.claude/agents/critic-*.md`). Step 11 previously consumed only Step 10's verdict labels. The candidate introduces a back-reference from a routing step to a content-classification step. Cycle: Step 7 produces scope-map; Step 8 challenges it; Step 9 generates against it; Step 10 critiques; Step 11 reads Step 7's content to decide replan. The cycle is not catastrophic but it is a new dependency edge across the workflow that the candidate does not draw.
- **Defense 2 couples the routing layer to a *string match on a filename pattern*.** "`.claude/agents/critic-*.md`" as a routing predicate is a fragile coupling: it triggers on critic-* changes specifically and on no other self-modifying change (e.g., a change to `requirement-classifier.md` or to CLAUDE.md itself is *also* self-modifying but does not match the pattern). The defense is named "replan-on-self-modification" but implements "replan-on-critic-modification." Naming-implementation drift.

## 4. Ignored architectural alternatives

1. **Orchestrator-as-sole-writer with structured return.** Critics return a typed structured object (verdict label + named sections); the orchestrator writes the file *verbatim* from the structured return, preserving every byte the critic produced. This preserves today's chokepoint (one writer), preserves ballot independence by construction (no filesystem path between critics), and addresses the user's audit-faithfulness motive (the file is the critic's bytes, just transcribed by a mechanical writer). The candidate dismisses this as "option E" with one sentence — that audit faithfulness via verbatim-copy is "structurally weaker" than agent-as-author. That dismissal is a *philosophical* claim about authorship, not a *structural* one. Architecturally, "verbatim copy by a mechanical writer" and "self-write" are equivalent on every property except attribution metadata.

2. **Append-only single-file panel log.** All three critics append to one shared file (`panel-log.md`) with structured section headers (`## architecture / verdict: ... / body: ...`). Single writer-target, single artifact, lens-isolation by section header rather than file boundary. This is what Google SRE Ch. 15 (canon-librarian §Direct fact 6 — "*one* shared document, not N sibling files") actually describes. The candidate does not engage with this contradicting passage — it cites canon's "filesystem-artifact" passage selectively.

3. **Synchronous critic-to-orchestrator return + asynchronous persistence by a dedicated `recorder` agent.** Critics return prose; a dedicated `recorder` agent (with `Write` and nothing else) persists each lens's bytes to disk. Separates judging from recording at the agent level. Reuses the Write-holder precedent without grafting authorship onto the reviewer.

## 5. Frame-level objection

The frame currently in play (`orchestrator parsimony, bounded by write-target boundary`, per `frame.md` §Revision 1) treats the critic-panel as a *pipeline of paraphrase-shaped jobs* in which the question is which jobs the orchestrator is doing that it shouldn't. **That frame is wrong because the panel is not a pipeline; it is a parliament.** A parliament's load-bearing property is not throughput-and-fidelity (pipeline metrics) — it is *each member's vote being formed independently of every other member's vote*, with a recording clerk who is structurally distinct from any voting member. The candidate's Defense 1 (instruction-level no-sibling-read) is a pipeline-frame defense applied to a parliament-frame problem: it tries to enforce vote-independence with a cognitive instruction inside the voter, when the parliament-frame solution is *to keep the voter and the clerk as different roles*. The frame error is upstream of the candidate; the candidate inherits it from `frame.md` Revision 1, which explicitly demoted the recursion/independence frame (Alt-3) and partially folded the boundary frame (Alt-2) back in only as a constraint. The candidate's Defense 2 (replan-on-self-modification) is a patch on the recursion blind spot but does not undo the frame demotion — it accepts the pipeline frame and adds a circuit-breaker. A parliament frame would not need that circuit-breaker because the clerk-voter separation would already prevent the rubber-stamping vector.

## 6. Verdict

**reject.**

What would change the verdict: a candidate that preserves single-writer (clerk-voter) separation — either via structured-return + orchestrator-verbatim-write (Alternative 1) or via a dedicated recorder agent (Alternative 3) — while still delivering byte-for-byte audit faithfulness. The role-fusion in the current candidate is the structural defect; adding `Write` to the reviewer is the wrong axis to optimize on.

# 03 — Reframe of the brief

## What the operator asked for

"Next-level upgrades that elevate the repo to another level of value." The framing optimises for **growth** — more capability, more value, a step up from the current plateau. Implicit assumption: the stack is at a plateau, and the move is *additive*.

## What I think the operator should also consider optimising for

**Closing the loop.** The stack today is a *forecasting machine without a calibration record*. It tells you where you are likely to fail before you fail; it never tells you whether the last hundred forecasts were right. Until that loop closes, every "next-level" addition compounds an error term you cannot measure. The single highest-value move may not be a new capability at all. It may be an instrument that tells the stack — and the operator — which of the existing capabilities are pulling weight and which are ceremony.

This is a reframe, not a refusal. Some of the long list is additive. But the deep dives weight toward **earning the right to grow** by first making the existing system *legible to itself*.

## The reframe in one sentence

The most valuable thing this stack can become is **not a better critic, but a better decision-archaeology-and-calibration substrate that happens to have a critic attached.** Restated in the operator's vocabulary: the value is less in the *next* recommendation it produces and more in the *trail* it leaves behind, scored over time against what the operator actually decided and how those decisions actually played out.

## Why this reframe is not just consolidation in disguise

A pure-consolidation reframe would say: *the stack is good, leave it alone, polish what's there.* That is too humble. The evidence is mixed:

- **Pro-additive:** the [Context7 session](../../.claude/session-artifacts/2026-04-26-context7-adoption-in-critic-stack/) shows the workflow producing dense, defensible, hard-to-anchor reasoning under a real adversarial test. The stack works on its terms. Adding capability where it works is not crazy.
- **Pro-measurement:** that same session ended with `Cap of two full loops reached… escalate to synthesis with residual disagreements named` — and there is no follow-up, ever, that asks whether the residual disagreements proved important six months later. The stack cannot tell its own success from its own theatre.
- **Pro-removal:** the three critic lenses share an authoring voice; the requirement-classifier runs first and produces a label that frequently does not change downstream behaviour; the subagent-distiller exists because subagent return contracts are loose. There is fat.

The reframe handles all three honestly: build the calibration substrate first (so additions and removals can be evaluated rather than asserted), then prune the fat (so the substrate is not measuring noise), then add capability (so additions land on a foundation that can score them).

## What this reframe forecloses, named

- **Big new agent rosters.** Adding three more agents on top of the existing ten without a measurement substrate makes the stack worse, not better. Any proposal in the long list that adds an agent without naming what it would be measured on is suspect.
- **Vector stores / RAG over session-artifacts.** Tempting and probably wrong as a first move. Cross-session retrieval before there is a calibrated *signal* about which sessions are valuable produces a noisier, not better, retrieval target.
- **Vibes-based "this is better" claims for any future version.** Once the calibration substrate exists, every subsequent change earns its keep against it or doesn't. This forecloses the temptation to ship "improvements" that feel right but were never tested.

## Proceeding under this frame

The long list (§04) is generated against this frame but not bound by it — diversity matters at the long-list stage. The shortlist (§05) selects under the frame. The deep dives (§07) commit to it. If the operator rejects the reframe at the shortlist stage, only the deep-dives need redoing; the long list still stands.

The brief also instructs me to *name the frame, do not bury it*. The frame is named here, in this file, and it is the only place where I argue *for* it as a frame rather than *from* it. Everything downstream is downstream of this choice.

## How to read this

- **First pass (~30 minutes):** [00](00-confirmation.md) → [03](03-reframe.md) → [05](05-shortlist.md) → [11](11-cheapest-experiment.md). Tells you the frame, the recommendations, and the move this week.
- **Second pass (~1 hour, sleep-on-it):** [01](01-reading-repo.md) → [02](02-reading-landscape.md) → [04](04-long-list.md) → [04a](04a-long-list-part-2.md) → [06](06-cuts.md). Tells you what I read into the repo and the field, the breadth I considered, and what I cut.
- **Third pass (~1.5 hours, decide-mode):** [07a](07-deepdive-decision-record-substrate.md) → [07b](07-deepdive-critic-collapse-and-ablation.md) → [07c](07-deepdive-inverted-librarian.md) → [08](08-sequencing.md) → [09](09-risks-to-the-plan.md) → [10](10-uncertainties.md). Tells you what would actually change, in what order, and what could go wrong.

## What this plan does and does not commit to

**Does:** name a frame and proceed under it; produce a long list, shortlist, cuts, and three deep dives; respect the brief's depth/breadth/novelty/taste bar; include at least one removal and at least one foundational reframe; surface contradicting evidence on every major proposal; name kill criteria and uncertainties.

**Does not:** edit any agent file, `CLAUDE.md`, the canon, or any session-artifact. The plan is strategic; building begins after the operator decides what to act on. The cheapest experiment in [11](11-cheapest-experiment.md) requires only a text editor and ~2.5 hours of operator time, and itself commits to nothing further.
